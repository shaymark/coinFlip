Why a coin flip?

For many people with ADHD, finishing a task is hard — the brain struggles to stay engaged without a clear payoff at the end.

A common trick is to reward yourself when you're done. It works… at first. But if the reward is guaranteed every time, the brain quickly gets used to it and the motivational spark fades.

Research on variable rewards suggests a better approach: after finishing a task, flip a coin. Heads — you get the treat. Tails — you don't.

Because the outcome is uncertain, your brain keeps releasing dopamine in anticipation. The surprise stays fresh, and the motivation keeps working. 🧠✨

Not medical advice — just a fun tool based on behavioral science.
